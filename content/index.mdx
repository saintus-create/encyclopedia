# Encyclopedia of Rhetoric and Composition

*Communication from Ancient Times to the Information Age*

Edited by **Theresa Enos**, University of Arizona

---

This one-volume reference work provides an introduction to rhetoric — including the major periods and personages, concepts and applications. Rhetoric, though the oldest and broadest of the humanities, draws on disciplines like anthropology, linguistics, philosophy, and psychology.

Browse 274 entries alphabetically using the sidebar, or use the search bar to find specific terms, figures, or concepts.

## How Entries Are Organized

Each entry takes one of four forms:

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Identification** | Brief definition of a figure, term, or concept |
| **Note** | Elaborated explanation of a key topic |
| **Essay** | In-depth exploration of a major subject |
| **Article** | Full treatment illuminating rhetoric's art and methodology |

Every entry includes a definition and discussion. Where available, entries also include a bibliography and cross-references to related entries under **See Also**.
